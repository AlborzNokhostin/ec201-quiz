Transversality Condition ‚Äî Week 2, Lecture 3-4 (October 16, 2025 ‚Äî Slides 16-20) ‚Ä¢ Added Nov 24, 3:33 PM by Alborz@Alborzs-MacBook-Pro (137.205.73.42)

Context: Explaining that debt doesn't have to be zero, but can't grow faster than interest rate

What She Said: "The condition doesn't say that debt has to be equal to zero as time goes to infinity. The condition says that the debt cannot grow faster than the interest rate, OK, because we will never see the country that is going to say, OK, the debt is 0, we settled everything. It's the growth rate of debt that is going to matter, and that's what the transversality condition is telling us."

Professor's Guarantee: "So if the debt grows faster than the interest rate, then you see that the transversality condition will not hold, and this is not going to be sustainable. OK, so this is very important. I'll probably put it on the test for sure, something, some version of this question."

What She's Talking About: Common misconception - transversality condition does NOT mean debt = 0 in the limit. It means present discounted value of debt ‚Üí 0, which happens when growth rate of debt < interest rate. She warns: if you answer "debt must be zero" on test, you'll get "-10 points"!
